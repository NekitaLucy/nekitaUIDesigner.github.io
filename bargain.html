Bargain Tracker

Timeframe

December 2022 - February 2023

Role:

Research, ideation, and design.

Skills applied for this project:

User-centred design, Prototyping, User research interviews, MVP document, Competitive analysis, User personas, User flows, Usability test plan, Preference testing, Responsive designs, Moodboard, Styleguide, UX research and UI design.

Tools: Figma

Purpose

The objective is to design a responsive web application for location-based recommendations that meets the requirements of its users and solves the problems they encounter with existing applications.

Problem statement

Research and design a responsive web app for a sales locator through a user-centric design process. Many apps need more personalization, making users receive irrelevant recommendations that align with their preferences and interests.

Solution

I used user research, interviews, user flows, A/B testing, real-time updates to the store, and a mobile and desktop experience to address the problems that users are experiencing.

UX DESIGN PHASE USER RESEARCH

User research

Conducting user research to help tailor the design of the UI for the responsive web app and defining the objectives of the user research using the who, what, when, where, why, and how framework.

Who are you designing for? ● For shoppers who want to save money

What kinds of tasks and goals will your audience be accomplishing as they use your website or app? ● Set up an account and set their location preferences with the distance. ● Select their price range for alerts. ● Set up a digital membership to gain points.

When will your audience be engaging with your product? When they have the day off or after work hours to shop.

Where will your audience be using what you’re designing? On any android or iphone mobile device.

Why is your audience choosing to use what you’ve designed? -They need to budget their expenses. -They want to save more money. -To compare the prices that different stores advertise for the same items.

How will your user accomplish their goals? -The user can save the promotions that interest them to look at a later time and compare. -The user can tap on the item to get the store’s information, address, and hours and to make a reservation.

Process

Proposal for a location-Based Recommendations Responsive Web App Brief: An online sales location finder based on the interests of the user, such as clothing, food, electronics, or services.

Search for sales near the user's location. A location finder web app can help users save money by going to stores near them and saving money on items they regularly buy or are interested in buying when promotions are available.
Preliminary ideas will be a filter feature for users to choose the types of sales/promotions they want to see on the map. For example, grocery stores and their items on sale, clothes, food, electronics, museums or services. Dates on how long the promotion/sales will be available.

Hypothesis for your research. What are you trying to prove or disprove?

Hypothesis 1: Budget shoppers are willing to shop more frequently and visit new stores if they can access information on promotions and deals nearby.

The user interviews.

Steps involved in the user interview process

  1. Preparing the interview script.
  2. Writing out 5-7 open questions to ask and additional questions about themselves and their interests.
  3. Keep detailed notes or record your sessions (with the interviewees' consent).
  4. Synthesize the research findings to process and reflect on the qualitative data received and relate it to the initial hypothesis.

Competitor analysis

2 to 3 competitor web apps on which to conduct a competitive analysis.
Competitor Overview, Marketing Profile, SWOT Analysis, and UX Analysis.

Competitor web app 1: Tiendeo

UX Analysis

Layout: The layout of the web app is seamless to navigate. The user can easily zoom in on the digital flyers once they tap on it.

Navigation structure: the menu bar has recognizable icons, and the categories of retailers are listed below the menu options, making it easy to change what type of shopping experience the user wants.

Compatibility: The web app is compatible with several browsers, especially Safari. If the user has an iPhone, it's more seamless as an app on the Safari browser. It's compatible with Chrome and Microsoft.

Call to action: they have a signup page for brands and retailers to work with them in digital marketing on the platform and the primary account for consumers to sign up and customize their shopping experience. The app can only activate the shopping list, cards, and notifications.

Competitor web app 2: Reebee

UX Analysis

Usability: the web app allows users to access the additional features and will enable users to sign in and create shopping lists.

Layout: the layout is simple to navigate. The menu dashboard has enough space for the shopping categories that users can choose from and slide to see more categories.

Differentiation: The feature they highlight in the menu is for users to create a shopping list, and the items are featured in their window when the user taps on an item, so the user doesn't have to zoom in on the flyer.
