Skin Remedy

Timeframe

March 2023 - April 2023

Role:

Research and design

Skills applied for this project:

Competitor Analysis, Adhering to iOS and Material Design guidelines, Mobile design patterns, haptic and sound wireframing, gesture annotations, Wireframing, User testing, and Prototyping.

Tools: Figma and Balsamiq

The app provides users with personalized recommendations, helping them navigate the complex world of skincare ingredients and find the perfect remedies for their skin.

Objective

To design a mobile app's iOS and Android versions that solve a specific user problem.

Solution

Annotate the UI elements and patterns in the Human Interface Guidelines (HIG) for iOS and Material Design for Android app guidelines between each design process to ensure compliance.

Process

Research phase

Who is the app for?

Skin remedy is for individuals seeking to enhance their skincare regimen and guarantee the compatibility and efficacy of their chosen products.

What kind of tasks will users complete as they use the app?

Enter their skin concerns and the types of products they use, and follow the guidelines on what products to combine and what to avoid combining.

Competitor Analysis

I analyzed similar apps about skincare to learn and gather ideas that touch on similar themes and compare iOS and Android versions where possible.

Think Dirty - Shop Clean

informs users of 40,000 allergens and ingredients to avoid, allowing them to scan their products' barcodes to shop for various skincare products.

Charm

The app gets users to input basic demographics about themselves and filters their concerns to create a daily log routine. In addition, the app has a section about facial, hair, feet, nails, lips, and body treatments.

My app and its features Skin Remedy: How does it stand out?

Simplify the process by informing users based on the ingredients they log in their profile, what hours of the day to use, how many times, and to suggest elements that will products amplify a benefit and which ones are incompatible.

Material design v ios Human interface

iOS and Android low-fidelity wireframes into a single PDF for side-by-side comparison. Include annotations about the key difference.

Mobile design patterns

Incorporating native design patterns from low-fidelity to mid-fidelity wireframes focusing on the layout of the visual hierarchy, and incorporating native design patterns.

Basic app structure

I categorized my screens based on primary, secondary, or edit screens to identify any missed screens to create designs for those missing screens then. Also, to identify any steps I may have overlooked and include those steps if necessary.

Sound and physical feedback

I evaluated how sounds and vibrations may enhance the app by listing which actions would benefit from sounds or vibrations.

Mobile Gestures

I annotated the wireframes to indicate the gestures users will need to use to interact with the UI by representing the intended gestures for each interaction in the wireframes and any helpful annotations.
